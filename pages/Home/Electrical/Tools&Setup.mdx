## Executive Summary

The FEDS 201 Electrical Subgroup operates as the central nervous system architect for our competitive robotics platform. Our responsibilities span from initial electrical design through competition-day troubleshooting, ensuring robust, safe, and reliable robot operation under demanding field conditions.

This operational manual serves as the comprehensive reference for all electrical systems, procedures, and standards employed by our team, incorporating lessons learned from the 2025 REEFSCAPES season and establishing protocols for future competitive cycles.

### Competition Day Pit Procedures

When the robot enters the pit area, follow this systematic approach:

**Step 1: Power Isolation**
- Disconnect main battery immediately
- Verify all stored energy is safely discharged
- Document any reported issues from drive team

**Step 2: Issue Assessment**
Common problems and initial diagnostics:
- **Robot-wide power loss:** Check battery connections, main breaker, SB connectors
- **Brownout conditions:** Verify fuse ratings, check for loose connections
- **Component intermittent failure:** Inspect CAN connections, power connections
- **Complete component failure:** Check fuse continuity, CAN bus integrity

**Step 3: Systematic Inspection**
- Visual inspection of all connections
- Continuity testing of suspected circuits  
- CAN bus diagnostic using appropriate tools
- Mechanical stress point examination

## üîå Connector Standards and Specifications

### High Current Connections (12-14 AWG)

**Anderson Powerpole Connectors:**
- 45A crimp terminals required
- Positive (red) on right, negative (black) on left when viewed from insertion point
- Used for motor controller connections and power extensions
- Proper crimping: No exposed wire beyond crimp, secure connection

**Ring Terminal Applications:**
- Direct PDH connections
- Motor controller power inputs (Kraken X60/X44)
- Battery terminal connections
- Proper installation: No insulation in crimp area, secure under terminal

### Low Current Connections (22-24 AWG)

**Molex SL Connector System:**
- Female connectors: CANcoder leads, component interconnects
- Male connectors: Device leads (Pigeon, sensors)
- Proper crimping: 2-click crimp positioning, ‚Öõ" wire strip

### Communication Connections

**Ethernet (RJ45) Connections:**
- T-568B wiring standard mandatory
- Custom length cables minimize wire loops
- 90-degree adapters for space-constrained installations
- Strain relief boots required

## üìä Troubleshooting Decision Matrix

### Rapid Diagnosis Flowchart

**Power Issues:**
1. No robot power ‚Üí Check battery voltage, main breaker, SB connections
2. Partial power loss ‚Üí Verify individual fuses, check PDH status LEDs
3. Brownout conditions ‚Üí Examine fuse ratings, look for high-resistance connections

**Communication Failures:**
1. CAN device non-responsive ‚Üí Check termination, verify CAN H/L connections
2. Radio disconnection ‚Üí Inspect ethernet cables, verify radio power
3. Sensor dropout ‚Üí Test sensor power, verify CAN ID conflicts

**Motor Control Problems:**
1. Motor non-responsive ‚Üí Check power connections, CAN communication, fuse continuity
2. Erratic motor behavior ‚Üí Inspect encoder connections, verify controller firmware
3. Overheating ‚Üí Check current draw, verify proper fuse rating, inspect ventilation

## üìñ Documentation Standards

### Wiring Diagram Requirements

All electrical systems must be documented with:
- Complete schematic showing all connections
- Wire gauge and color specifications
- Connector type and pin assignments
- Fuse/breaker ratings and locations
- CAN ID assignments
- Power consumption calculations

### Labeling Convention

**Wire Labeling:**
- Format: [Source]-[Destination]-[Signal Type]
- Example: "PDH-01-MOTOR-FL" (PDH channel 1 to front-left motor)
- Color-coded by function (Red: +12V, Black: Ground, Yellow: CAN-H, Green: CAN-L)

**Component Labeling:**
- Unique identifier for each device
- QR codes for quick access to documentation
- Maintenance log attachment point

### Competition Documentation Package

Required documents for competition:
1. Complete robot electrical schematic
2. Component specification sheets
3. Troubleshooting quick reference
4. Emergency contact information
5. Spare parts inventory list

**Reliability First:**
- Minimize connection points
- Use appropriate strain relief
- Plan for thermal management
- Implement redundancy for critical systems

**Maintainability:**
- Accessible connection points
- Clear labeling and documentation
- Modular design for easy replacement
- Standardized connector types

**Safety Always:**
- Proper fuse protection
- Secure wire routing
- Emergency disconnect accessibility
- Regular inspection schedules
